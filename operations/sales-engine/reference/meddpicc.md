# MEDDPICC Qualification Methodology

> The qualification framework used to evaluate every deal in the pipeline.
> Load at boot. Deeper reference loaded by closer during deal reviews.

## Overview

MEDDPICC is an 8-letter qualification framework. Each letter represents a
critical dimension of deal health. A deal with gaps in any letter is at risk.

## The 8 Letters

### M -- Metrics

**What**: The quantified business impact of solving the buyer's problem.

**Why it matters**: No metrics = no business case = no budget approval. The buyer
needs to justify the investment internally. If they can't quantify the ROI, the
deal dies in committee.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- buyer hasn't articulated the impact
- Level 1: Identified -- buyer acknowledges a problem exists
- Level 2: Validated -- buyer has a rough number ("it costs us a lot")
- Level 3: Confirmed -- buyer has specific metrics ("$500K/year in lost productivity")

**Key questions**:
- "What does this problem cost you today -- in dollars, time, or risk?"
- "How do you measure success for this initiative?"
- "If you solve this, what does it unlock downstream?"

### E -- Economic Buyer

**What**: The person who can say "yes" and authorize the spend.

**Why it matters**: If you're selling to someone who can't sign, you're not selling.
You're presenting. The economic buyer may not be in every meeting, but they must be
identified, accessed, and aligned.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- don't know who approves budget
- Level 1: Identified -- named person, but no direct access
- Level 2: Validated -- met or confirmed through champion
- Level 3: Confirmed -- direct meeting, they've expressed support

**Key questions**:
- "Who ultimately signs off on investments like this?"
- "Walk me through how budget gets allocated for this type of project."
- "Is there anyone who could veto this even if everyone else agrees?"

### D -- Decision Criteria

**What**: The specific requirements the buyer will evaluate solutions against.

**Why it matters**: If you don't know the criteria, you can't shape them. If you
can't shape them, the competitor who does will win.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no criteria articulated
- Level 1: Identified -- buyer listed some requirements
- Level 2: Validated -- criteria ranked by priority
- Level 3: Confirmed -- formal evaluation matrix shared

**Key questions**:
- "What are the top 3 things you're evaluating solutions on?"
- "Are there must-haves vs. nice-to-haves?"
- "How will you compare vendors side by side?"

### D -- Decision Process

**What**: The steps from today to signed contract -- who's involved, what approvals
are needed, and what timeline they're working against.

**Why it matters**: Deals don't close on your timeline. They close on the buyer's
process. If you don't map it, you'll forecast wrong and miss your window.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no visibility into process
- Level 1: Identified -- general understanding ("we'll evaluate a few vendors")
- Level 2: Validated -- step-by-step process with named stakeholders
- Level 3: Confirmed -- mutual action plan agreed with dates

**Key questions**:
- "Walk me through the typical buying process here."
- "Who's involved at each stage?"
- "Have you bought something similar recently? How long did it take?"

### P -- Paper Process

**What**: The procurement, legal, and contract mechanics that happen between
verbal agreement and signed contract.

**Why it matters**: This is where deals go to die. Legal review, security
questionnaire, vendor registration, procurement approval -- each adds weeks.
If you don't map this early, your close date is fiction.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no visibility into procurement
- Level 1: Identified -- "legal will need to review"
- Level 2: Validated -- specific steps with estimated timelines
- Level 3: Confirmed -- procurement requirements met or in progress

**Key questions**:
- "Once we agree on terms, what's the contract process?"
- "Does legal need to review? How long does that typically take?"
- "Any procurement requirements (security review, vendor registration)?"

### I -- Identify Pain

**What**: The specific, urgent problem that's driving the buyer to act NOW.

**Why it matters**: No pain = no urgency = no deal. "Exploring options" is not
pain. Pain is "we lost $200K last quarter because our system went down twice."

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no pain articulated
- Level 1: Identified -- problem acknowledged in general terms
- Level 2: Validated -- specific pain with business impact
- Level 3: Confirmed -- pain is acute, has executive sponsor, and has budget

**Key questions**:
- "What's happening today that made you take this meeting?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?"
- "Is this a 'hair on fire' problem or a 'nice to fix' problem?"

### C -- Champion

**What**: An internal advocate who has power, access, and personal motivation
to push the deal forward.

**Why it matters**: You can't be in the room for every internal discussion.
Your champion sells for you when you're not there. Without one, you're
outsourcing your fate to chance.

**The 3 tests**: A real champion passes ALL three:
1. **Power**: Can they influence the economic buyer?
2. **Access**: Will they get you meetings with other stakeholders?
3. **Motivation**: Do they personally win if this deal happens?

If any test fails, they're a coach (useful but insufficient), not a champion.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no internal advocate identified
- Level 1: Identified -- friendly contact, but power/access/motivation unclear
- Level 2: Validated -- passes 2 of 3 tests
- Level 3: Confirmed -- passes all 3, actively advocating

### C -- Competition

**What**: Who else is in the deal, and how the buyer perceives the alternatives.

**Why it matters**: There's always competition -- even if it's "do nothing."
If you don't know who you're competing against, you can't position against them.

**Evidence levels**:
- Level 0: Unknown -- no visibility into alternatives
- Level 1: Identified -- know who else is being evaluated
- Level 2: Validated -- understand competitor strengths/weaknesses in buyer's eyes
- Level 3: Confirmed -- have shaped evaluation criteria to favor us

**Key questions**:
- "Who else are you evaluating?"
- "What do you like about their approach?"
- "What concerns do you have about them?"

## Scoring

Each letter scored 0-3. Total possible: 24 points.

| Score | Percentage | Assessment |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| 20-24 | 83-100% | Strong -- high probability close |
| 15-19 | 63-79% | Moderate -- winnable with focused action |
| 10-14 | 42-58% | Weak -- significant gaps, deal at risk |
| 0-9 | 0-38% | Critical -- consider disqualifying |

## Stage Gate Requirements

| Stage | Minimum MEDDPICC | Required Letters at Level 2+ |
|-------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Discovery -> Demo | 60% (14/24) | I (Pain), E (Economic Buyer) |
| Demo -> Proposal | 70% (17/24) | + C (Champion), D (Decision Criteria) |
| Proposal -> Negotiate | 80% (19/24) | + D (Decision Process), P (Paper Process) |
| Negotiate -> Close | 90% (22/24) | All letters at Level 2+, M and C at Level 3 |
