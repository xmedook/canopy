# Email Sequence Templates

> Proven outreach sequences by scenario. Load on-demand when building campaigns.

## Sequence 1: Standard Cold Outbound (21-Day)

### Target: ICP Score 7+, no prior relationship

| Day | Channel | Subject/Action | Template |
|-----|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Email | {Observation about their business} | Cold Open |
| 3 | LinkedIn | Connect + personalized note | LinkedIn Connect |
| 5 | Email | Re: {original subject} | Value-Add Follow-Up |
| 8 | Phone | Call referencing emails | Call Script A |
| 10 | Email | {Relevant content/benchmark} | Content Share |
| 14 | LinkedIn | Engage with their content + DM | LinkedIn Engage |
| 17 | Email | {Similar company result} | Social Proof |
| 21 | Email | {Break-up} | Graceful Exit |

### Templates

**Cold Open**
```
Subject: {Company}'s {specific observation}

{First name},

Noticed {specific detail from research -- recent hire, product launch, funding, job posting}.
{One sentence connecting that observation to a problem we solve.}

{Similar company} was dealing with the same thing -- {one sentence about their outcome}.

{Question that's easy to answer and opens dialogue}?

{Signature}
```

**Value-Add Follow-Up**
```
Subject: Re: {original subject}

{First name},

Sent this last week -- wanted to add one thing.

{Relevant data point, industry benchmark, or article link that's useful
regardless of whether they buy from us.}

{Restate CTA from email 1, slightly different phrasing.}

{Signature}
```

**Social Proof**
```
Subject: How {similar company} solved {problem}

{First name},

{Similar company in their industry} was facing {problem similar to prospect's}.
In {timeframe}, they {specific quantified result}.

The thing that made the difference was {differentiator -- not a feature, an approach}.

Worth 20 minutes to see if the same approach fits {prospect company}?

{Signature}
```

**Graceful Exit**
```
Subject: Should I close your file?

{First name},

I've reached out a few times and haven't heard back -- totally fine,
I know {relevant role} is a busy job.

Two possibilities:
1. This isn't a priority right now (no hard feelings, I'll check back in 6 months)
2. I missed the mark on relevance (happy to hear what would be more useful)

Either way, I don't want to be the annoying vendor in your inbox.

{Signature}
```

## Sequence 2: Warm Introduction (14-Day)

### Target: Referred by mutual connection or inbound interest

| Day | Channel | Subject/Action | Template |
|-----|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Email | {Mutual connection} suggested we connect | Warm Intro |
| 3 | LinkedIn | Connect referencing intro | LinkedIn Warm |
| 5 | Email | Quick follow-up with value | Warm Follow-Up |
| 7 | Phone | Call with intro reference | Call Script B |
| 10 | Email | Relevant case study | Warm Social Proof |
| 14 | Email | Final touch | Warm Close |

### Templates

**Warm Intro**
```
Subject: {Mutual connection name} suggested we connect

{First name},

{Connection name} mentioned you're {working on X / dealing with Y / exploring Z}
and thought it'd be worth us connecting.

{One sentence about why -- tied to their specific situation.}

{Connection} thought so because {reason -- what we did for connection or similar}.

Open to a quick call this week?

{Signature}

P.S. {Connection name} said I could mention {specific detail that builds credibility}.
```

## Sequence 3: Re-engagement (10-Day)

### Target: Previously interested prospect who went dark

| Day | Channel | Subject/Action | Template |
|-----|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Email | {New development relevant to them} | Re-engage Open |
| 4 | LinkedIn | Share relevant article | LinkedIn Re-engage |
| 7 | Email | {New case study or product update} | Re-engage Value |
| 10 | Email | Check-in with easy out | Re-engage Close |

### Templates

**Re-engage Open**
```
Subject: {New thing relevant to their situation}

{First name},

We spoke {timeframe} ago about {topic}. Wanted to flag something new.

{New development -- product update, case study, industry shift --
that's relevant to what they cared about.}

Has anything changed on your end with {their initiative}?

{Signature}
```

## Sequence 4: Event/Trigger-Based (7-Day)

### Target: Company just had a trigger event (funding, hire, launch)

| Day | Channel | Subject/Action | Template |
|-----|---------|---------------|----------|
| 1 | Email | Congrats + insight | Trigger Open |
| 3 | LinkedIn | Connect + congrats | LinkedIn Trigger |
| 5 | Email | How similar companies handled this phase | Trigger Follow-Up |
| 7 | Email | Specific offer to help | Trigger Close |

### Templates

**Trigger Open**
```
Subject: Congrats on {trigger event}

{First name},

Saw the news about {specific trigger -- funding round, new VP hire, product launch}.
Exciting move.

{One sentence about what typically happens next in companies at this stage
-- the challenge that follows the trigger.}

{Question about how they're approaching that challenge.}

{Signature}
```

## Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Target | Good | Needs Work |
|--------|--------|------|-----------|
| Open Rate | 40%+ | 30-39% | < 30% |
| Reply Rate | 8%+ | 5-7% | < 5% |
| Meeting Rate | 3%+ | 1.5-2.9% | < 1.5% |
| Unsubscribe Rate | < 1% | 1-2% | > 2% |

**If open rate is low**: Subject lines need work. Test shorter, more specific.
**If open rate is high but reply rate is low**: Body copy isn't resonating. Test different angles.
**If reply rate is high but meeting rate is low**: CTA is wrong. Test lower-friction asks.

## Timing Best Practices

| Time Slot | Open Rate Index | Best For |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Tue-Thu 8-10am local | 1.2x | Cold outreach to executives |
| Mon/Fri 7-8am local | 1.1x | Follow-ups (catch early inbox scanners) |
| Tue-Wed 2-4pm local | 1.0x | Content shares (afternoon reading) |
| Weekends | 0.6x | Avoid for B2B |

Send in the prospect's timezone, not yours.
