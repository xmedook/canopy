# Brand Voice Guide

> The definitive reference for how we sound. Load at boot.
> Every piece of content must pass this guide before publication.

## Voice Attributes

### We Are
| Attribute | What It Means | Example |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Clear** | We say things simply. No jargon unless the audience uses it. | "This helps you rank higher" not "This improves your SERP positioning" |
| **Confident** | We know what we're talking about. No hedging. | "Do this" not "You might want to consider doing this" |
| **Helpful** | Every piece of content teaches something useful. | Lead with value, not self-promotion |
| **Honest** | We admit limitations and trade-offs. | "This works for X but not for Y" |
| **Human** | We write like people talk. Natural, not robotic. | Contractions are fine. Personality is welcome. |

### We Are Not
| Attribute | What It Sounds Like | Fix |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| **Academic** | Passive voice, long sentences, unnecessary jargon | Active voice, short sentences, plain words |
| **Salesy** | "Amazing", "incredible", "revolutionary", forced urgency | Specific claims with evidence |
| **Corporate** | "Leverage synergies", "best-in-class", "mission-critical" | Plain English alternatives |
| **Robotic** | No personality, generic advice, could be any company | Add specific examples, opinions, and experience |
| **Preachy** | Talking down, over-explaining obvious things | Respect the reader's intelligence |

## Vocabulary Rules

### Preferred Words
| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Utilize | Use |
| Leverage | Use, apply, build on |
| Synergy | Collaboration, combined effect |
| Innovative | Specific: "saves 40% of time", "automates X" |
| Best-in-class | Specific: "highest in [benchmark]", "top-rated on [platform]" |
| Game-changer | Describe the actual change |
| Disruptive | Describe what it actually changes |
| Scalable | Describe the actual scaling: "handles 10x more traffic" |
| Seamless | Describe the experience: "takes 2 clicks to set up" |

### Banned Phrases
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "Without further ado..."
- "Let's dive in / deep dive"
- "At the end of the day..."
- "Moving the needle"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Circle back"
- "Just checking in"
- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "In conclusion..."
- "As you know..."

### Technical Terms
Use technical terms when writing for a technical audience. Always use them correctly.
If a term might be unfamiliar, define it on first use -- once. Don't over-explain.

## Tone Calibration

Tone adjusts by context while voice stays constant:

| Context | Tone Shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Blog post | Conversational, educational | "Here's what we found..." |
| Case study | Factual, results-focused | "After 6 months, organic traffic increased 340%." |
| Social media | Energetic, punchy | "Your content strategy is broken. Here's the fix." |
| Newsletter | Personal, insider | "Three things caught my eye this week..." |
| Guide/Tutorial | Clear, step-by-step | "Step 1: Open your analytics dashboard." |
| Thought leadership | Opinionated, forward-looking | "The content marketing playbook is changing. Here's how." |

## Grammar and Style

### Sentence Structure
- Average sentence length: 15-20 words
- Mix short (5-10) and long (20-30). Never 3+ long sentences in a row.
- Active voice default. Passive voice only when actor is unknown or irrelevant.
- Lead sentences with the important information.

### Paragraph Structure
- Max 3-4 sentences per paragraph
- One idea per paragraph
- White space is our friend -- don't wall-of-text

### Formatting
- Use headers for scanability (H2 for sections, H3 for sub-sections)
- Use bullet lists for 3+ parallel items
- Use numbered lists for sequential steps
- Bold key terms on first use
- Italics for emphasis sparingly (once per section max)

### Punctuation
- Oxford comma: yes, always
- Em dash: use for emphasis and asides -- like this
- Exclamation marks: zero in blog posts and professional content. Rarely in social.
- Ellipsis: avoid in professional content. Acceptable in social/casual contexts.

### Numbers
- Spell out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Always use numerals in data/stats: "increased by 3x"
- Use % symbol, not "percent"

## Reading Level Target

- **Blog posts**: 8th grade (Flesch-Kincaid)
- **Social media**: 6th grade
- **Technical guides**: 10th grade (audience expects precision)
- **Email/newsletter**: 7th grade

Test with Hemingway Editor or equivalent. Target "Good" or "OK" score.

## Brand Personality in Action

### Introducing a concept:
**No**: "It has come to our attention that many organizations are failing to leverage the full potential of their content marketing strategies."
**Yes**: "Most content strategies fail. Here's the pattern we see -- and how to fix it."

### Explaining something complex:
**No**: "The algorithmic considerations of search engine optimization necessitate a multi-faceted approach to keyword integration."
**Yes**: "Google wants to show the best answer. Your job is to create it. Here's how keywords help."

### Sharing results:
**No**: "Our innovative solution enabled transformative results for the client."
**Yes**: "Organic traffic went from 5K to 50K monthly visits in 6 months. Here's what we changed."
